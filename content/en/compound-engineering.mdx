# Compound engineering

The first time you solve a problem with Claude, you save maybe an hour. The second time the same problem shows up, if you did nothing in between, you save another hour. Linear.

Compound engineering is what turns linear into exponential. Each time you solve something, you leave behind a reusable piece. Next time the problem shows up — or something in its neighborhood — that piece already exists. Your agent is more capable than it was yesterday.

## The three steps

**1. Handle it once.** Just solve the problem. Don't try to generalize yet. Ship the fix.

**2. Turn it into a skill.** When the pattern shows up again, stop. Write the steps down as a skill — a `SKILL.md` with instructions, plus any helper files. Put it in `~/.claude/skills/`.

**3. Turn skills into plugins.** When three or four related skills cluster together, bundle them as a plugin. Now your team can install the whole pattern in one command.

This is the progression. Problem → skill → plugin → reuse. Repeat.

## What makes a good skill

A skill is well-shaped when:

- It solves a specific, named problem
- The steps are repeatable, not tacit
- A teammate could install it and use it without asking you how

A skill is badly shaped when:

- It's too generic ("helps with anything")
- It duplicates an existing skill with small tweaks
- It depends on secrets in your head

Write skills after you've done something twice. Not after once (premature), not after ten times (you've wasted eight iterations).

## The plugin level

Plugins bundle related skills. They're the unit of sharing.

If you build workshop content: bundle a `brainstorm`, `plan`, `work`, `review`, and `compound` skill into one plugin. Now your team can install the whole workflow with `/plugin install`.

This is what the [Compound Engineering plugin](https://github.com/every-to/compound-engineering) does — bundles the full loop. Read its source for shape inspiration.

## Real example

Someone on your team writes a good postmortem. Once. Next incident, nobody remembers the structure.

- **Handle once:** write the postmortem. Ship.
- **Skill:** extract the structure into `~/.claude/skills/postmortem/SKILL.md` with sections, prompts, and rejection criteria. Next postmortem: Claude guides the author.
- **Plugin:** bundle `postmortem`, `incident-review`, `on-call-handoff` into an incident-response plugin. Rolls out across engineering.

That's compound. The first postmortem took four hours. The tenth takes forty minutes and is better.
